Night School x Precog workshops
October 5:30 PM – 8:30 PM

Night School is an inter-disciplinary workshop hosted by the Yale Graphic Design department, which brings a lineup of guest artists, theorists and researchers to work together with students in the program. Night School sessions create a space for multi-level engagements between the cohorts around critical, timely issues, developing meaningful discourse between students and faculty and an expansion beyond the walls of the department. As such, Night School offers a space for conviviality, alternative pedagogy and co-curricular thinking that in turn informs the continued development of the department, program and wider practice.

This year’s Night School is designed in conversation with Precog, a New York-based independently edited and published magazine that explores art, techno plastics, cyber culture, and feminism. Founded in 2015, Precog has published 8 issues and worked with close to 300 artists and writers on magazines, public programs, and exhibitions. Precog supports expansive ideas about gender and materiality, and seeks to publish a diverse range of artists and writers across race and gender identities, including many international contributors. As a method and in its submissions, Precog emphasizes creative play, research, and giving artists opportunities to explore new ideas.

In applying for a Belonging at Yale Grant we are seeking funds for guest facilitators for a range of workshops, to help bring a diversity of outside voices into the department and shape the workshop. Guest facilitators will work in a more intimate setting of 10–12 students as well as coming together with a wider public. These sessions will be open to the students in all years of the Graduate Graphic Design program and to Undergraduate Seniors majoring in Graphic Design. The workshops and engagement with Precog will also shape and become a key component of the forthcoming Volume 9 issue of the magazine, extending the workshop activities, discussions, production and exploration to an international public making use of the international distribution and circulation of Precog Magazine.
